History of Pixel Art

Pixel art was widely used in early video games, bringing life to characters and worlds through simple square pixels. Some of the most iconic games include:

  • Super Mario Bros.
  • Pac-Man
  • Space Invaders
  • The Legend of Zelda
  • Sonic the Hedgehog
  • Donkey Kong
  • Metroid
  • Final Fantasy
  • Street Fighter II
  • Mega Man
  • Tetris
  • Castlevania

As technology advanced, the limitations of pixel art pushed artists to be creative with minimal resources. Today, pixel art is celebrated as both an aesthetic and a nostalgic nod to the past. It is widely used in indie games, digital art projects, and even modern digital design, continuing to inspire artists across the world.
